\noindent
Goal: Let $c$ be a constant. Use a physics-informed neural network (PINN) to solve the second-order linear non-homogeneous ordinary differential equation
\begin{equation}
    c u'' + u' = x, \quad x \in (0,1)
\end{equation}
subject to the boundary conditions
\begin{equation}
    u(0) = 0, \quad u(1) = 1
\end{equation}

\noindent
Task 1: Prove that the exact solution of the ODE is, 
for $c \neq 0$,
\begin{equation}
            u(x) = \left( \frac{c + \frac{1}{2}}{1 - e^{-\frac{1}{c}}} \right) \left( 1 - e^{-\frac{x}{c}} \right) + \frac{1}{2}x^2 - cx
\end{equation}

\noindent
Task 2: What is the exact solution of the ODE when $c = 0$?

\noindent
Task 3: Implement a PINN to approximate the solution of the ODE when $c \neq 0$, and compare the numerical approximation with the exact solution.
